Understanding Demographic Bias in Facial Recognition Systems

Exploring fairness, accuracy, and equity in modern biometric identification technologies

Project Overview

Research Goal

Investigating how facial recognition technology performs across different demographic groups and identifying sources of algorithmic bias.

Methodology

Comprehensive analysis of accuracy metrics, false positive rates, and system performance variations across age, gender, and ethnicity.

Impact

Contributing to more equitable and fair AI systems that serve all communities without discrimination or disproportionate error rates.

Research Focus Areas

Algorithm Performance Analysis

Evaluating how different facial recognition algorithms respond to diverse facial features and characteristics across populations.

Training Data Representation

Examining the composition and diversity of datasets used to train facial recognition models and their impact on outcomes.

Real-world Deployment Implications

Understanding the societal consequences when biased systems are deployed in law enforcement, security, and commercial applications.
